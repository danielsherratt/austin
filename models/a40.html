Model: A40

History

The A40 nameplate covered a range of models starting with the A40 Devon and Dorset, followed by the A40 Somerset, and later the A40 Farina (designed by Pininfarina). The A40 introduced post-war styling and practicality.


Key generations:

  • A40 Devon/Dorset (1947–1952) – Sturdy saloons with 1.2L engines.
  • A40 Somerset (1952–1954) – More rounded styling, improved features.
  • A40 Farina (1958–1967) – A hatchback-like body with Italian design influence, foreshadowing modern small cars.


The Farina model was especially popular in New Zealand, often assembled locally from CKD kits. It was appreciated for its economy and was widely used as a family or fleet car.
