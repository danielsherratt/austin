Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin 60 was launched in 1908 as one of the most powerful and prestigious cars of its time. This model was designed for those who sought the ultimate in luxury and performance, with its large engine and refined features. The Austin 60 was targeted at elite customers, including aristocrats, business leaders, and those in need of executive transport.

Produced in limited numbers, the Austin 60 became a symbol of luxury and was used in the early days of automotive racing, highlighting its power and durability.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 6-cylinder, side-valve
  • Displacement: Approximately 9.6 liters
  • Power Output: 60 horsepower
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Chassis: Reinforced, heavy-duty frame for enhanced stability
  • Weight: Approximately 1,800 kg (3,968 lbs)

The Austin 60 was engineered to perform at high speeds, with a robust 6-cylinder engine that could handle long-distance travel and intense competition. Its chassis was designed to support the car's powerful engine while providing comfort and stability for passengers.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin 60 Saloon: The primary variant, offering a luxurious closed-body design for high-end clients.
  • Austin 60 Tourer: An open-top model popular for those seeking a combination of luxury and outdoor driving experience.

Due to its limited production run, the Austin 60 was a rare sight, even among luxury vehicles, and was seen as a top-tier model in the early 20th century.

The Austin 60 in New Zealand

The Austin 60, due to its rarity and high cost, was a luxury vehicle used by New Zealand's elite in the early 20th century. It served as a status symbol and was often used for special occasions, government purposes, or by businesses needing top-quality transport.

Today, the Austin 60 is a prized collector's item in New Zealand, with only a few models remaining in the country, cherished by vintage car enthusiasts and historical vehicle preservationists.
