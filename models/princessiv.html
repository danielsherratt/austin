Austin Princess IV (1956–1968)

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin Princess IV was introduced in 1956 as a luxury saloon car, offering a blend of comfort, style, and performance. It was part of Austin’s prestigious Princess range, designed to cater to the needs of high-end customers, including business executives and dignitaries. The Princess IV was an evolution of earlier models in the Princess series, featuring more modern styling, a more powerful engine, and improved interior comfort.

The Princess IV was built with a focus on providing an exceptional driving experience, both for drivers and passengers. It was often used as a chauffeur-driven vehicle, thanks to its luxurious interior and smooth performance. The car became synonymous with prestige and elegance in the 1950s and 1960s, making it one of the most sought-after models in the British luxury market.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 6-cylinder, inline
  • Displacement: Approximately 3.0 liters
  • Power Output: 102 horsepower
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Chassis: Sturdy monocoque construction with a long wheelbase
  • Weight: Approximately 1,600 kg (3,527 lbs)

The Princess IV was powered by a 6-cylinder engine that provided smooth and reliable performance for its time. Its chassis was designed for comfort, with a longer wheelbase ensuring ample legroom and a smooth ride. The car’s interior featured high-quality materials, providing an elegant and luxurious environment for its passengers.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin Princess IV Saloon: The standard luxury version of the car, offering comfortable seating for four to six passengers.
  • Austin Princess IV Limousine: A stretched version of the saloon, designed for official and formal occasions, often used as a chauffeur-driven vehicle.

The Princess IV was available in two main variants: the saloon, which was the most common version, and the limousine, which provided additional space for passengers. The limousine version was typically used by those in need of more luxury and space, including for ceremonial and executive purposes.

The Austin Princess IV in New Zealand

The Austin Princess IV was imported to New Zealand in limited numbers, where it was considered a top-tier luxury vehicle. It was favored by business executives and high-profile individuals who required a prestigious car for both personal and official use. The Princess IV became a symbol of status and elegance in the country during the 1950s and 1960s.

Today, the Austin Princess IV remains a rare and sought-after classic in New Zealand, with a few well-preserved examples being showcased at vintage car events and preserved by collectors. It continues to be regarded as one of the finest British luxury saloons of its era.
