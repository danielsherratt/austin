Austin 7HP (1910)

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin 7HP, introduced in 1910, was a small and affordable car designed to cater to the mass market. It featured a simple, yet effective design, making it one of the first affordable cars for the general public in the UK and abroad, including New Zealand. The 7HP was a precursor to the legendary Austin 7, which would go on to revolutionize motoring in the 1920s.

The Austin 7HP played an important role in motorizing the masses, as it was a compact and economical alternative to larger, more expensive vehicles.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 2-cylinder, side-valve
  • Displacement: 696 cc
  • Power Output: 7 horsepower
  • Transmission: 2-speed manual
  • Weight: Approximately 450 kg (992 lbs)

The Austin 7HP was an entry-level vehicle, making it accessible to a large portion of the population. Despite its small size, it was a reliable and efficient car, ideal for city commuting and short-distance travel.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin 7HP Chummy: An open-top version, designed for those who wanted a compact and affordable car.

The 7HP marked the beginning of Austin's journey into affordable motoring. Its simple design and reliability made it a popular choice for everyday use.

The Austin 7HP in New Zealand

The Austin 7HP was one of the first cars to be widely adopted in New Zealand. Its affordability made it accessible to a broader segment of the population, and it played a key role in making car ownership more common in the early 20th century.

Many Austin 7HPs were sold in New Zealand, particularly in urban areas, and it became a beloved part of the country's motoring history. Today, the Austin 7HP is an important piece of New Zealand's automotive heritage.
