Austin A55 Cambridge (1957–1969)

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin A55 Cambridge was introduced in 1957 as part of Austin’s successful range of compact and reliable family cars. The A55 was an update to the A40 Cambridge and marked a shift towards a more modern design and improved comfort features. With its larger body and more powerful engine, the A55 Cambridge became a popular choice for families and small businesses looking for an affordable yet stylish vehicle.

The A55 was available in both saloon and estate (station wagon) variants, and it was one of the top-selling cars in Britain during the late 1950s and 1960s. The vehicle was also exported to various countries, including New Zealand, where it became a staple on local roads.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 4-cylinder, inline
  • Displacement: Approximately 1.5 liters
  • Power Output: 55 horsepower
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Chassis: Monocoque design with better suspension for a smooth ride
  • Weight: Approximately 950 kg (2,094 lbs)

The A55 Cambridge was equipped with a 4-cylinder engine, offering a good balance of power and fuel efficiency. The vehicle’s design emphasized comfort, with a suspension system that provided a smoother ride compared to previous models. The monocoque construction made the car more durable and stable, ideal for both urban and rural driving.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin A55 Cambridge Saloon: The standard version, offering ample space and comfort for families.
  • Austin A55 Cambridge Estate: A more practical station wagon variant designed for families or businesses needing more cargo space.

The A55 was offered in two main variants: the saloon and the estate (station wagon). The saloon was the most popular version, providing comfort and space for daily driving, while the estate variant offered additional cargo room, making it ideal for transporting goods or large families.

The Austin A55 Cambridge in New Zealand

The A55 Cambridge was widely popular in New Zealand, where it became a favorite choice for families and small businesses due to its affordability, practicality, and reliability. The car’s spacious interior and good performance made it suitable for both urban and rural environments, where it was used for a variety of purposes, including commuting, family trips, and even light commercial work.

Today, the Austin A55 Cambridge remains a cherished classic in New Zealand. Many well-maintained examples are still in use, and the model is a popular entry in classic car shows and vintage vehicle events.
