History

The Montego was introduced by British Leyland under the Austin Rover brand and was one of the last cars to wear the Austin name. It was available as a saloon and estate, with 1.3L to 2.0L petrol and diesel engines.


  • Known for roomy interiors and good ride quality.
  • Faced stiff competition from Japanese imports.
  • Turbo variants were introduced with sportier performance.


Though available in New Zealand, the Montego struggled to match the reliability and refinement of competitors like the Toyota Corolla or Honda Accord. Its popularity waned quickly, and it marked the end of the Austin badge in both the UK and export markets.
