History

Launched in 1959, the Mini was a revolutionary small car designed by Sir Alec Issigonis. It introduced front-wheel drive, a transverse engine, and a compact, boxy layout that maximized interior space.


Key Variants:

  • Austin Seven (1959) – The first Mini, renamed the Austin Mini in 1962.
  • Mini Cooper (1961+) – Performance variants co-developed with racing legend John Cooper.
  • Mini Clubman (1969) – Squared-off front styling.
  • Mini 1275GT – Sporty variant with a tachometer and larger engine.


In New Zealand, local assembly began in the 1960s, and Minis became iconic for their affordability and fuel efficiency. They were popular across all demographics, from students to businesses. The Mini remains a beloved classic in NZ today.
