Austin Seven

Basic Info

Model: Seven

Production: 1922 - 1939

Engine: 0.7L 12 horsepower

Parts SKU: seven

History

The Austin 7 is arguably Austin’s most influential model. Launched in 1922, it was one of the first truly affordable small cars and became known as the "Baby Austin." Designed by Herbert Austin and engineer Stanley Edge, it featured a 747cc side-valve engine and was available as a tourer, saloon, van, or sports model.

1922 – Austin 7 Introduced

Model: "Type A" Austin Seven
First production model, designed by Sir Herbert Austin and Stanley Edge.
696cc side-valve engine, 10.5 hp.

1923–1926 – Early Variants

  • Chummy Tourer: Very popular open-top 4-seater
  • Box Saloon: Enclosed body version
  • Cup Model (1924–1926): Lightweight sports variant for racing

1926–1930 – Growth & Evolution

  • Engine increased to 747cc in 1926
  • More body styles: Top Hat Saloon, Clifton, Fabric-bodied saloons
  • Electric starter becomes standard in 1927
  • Improved gearbox and body from 1928

1931–1934 – Updated Styling

  • Austin 7 Swallow: Custom body by William Lyons (later Jaguar)
  • Box Saloon Mk II: Steel-bodied, more refined
  • Nippy & Ulster: Sporty two-seaters for racing

1934–1939 – Ruby Era

  • Ruby Mk I (1934): Curved grille, modern saloon styling
  • Pearl: Luxury 2-door saloon
  • Opal: Compact 2-seater tourer
  • Ruby Mk II (1936): Larger windows, better gearbox, improved brakes

1939 – End of Production

Production of the Austin 7 ends. Over 290,000 units made.
Followed by the Austin 8. Its legacy influenced the post-war Mini.


The Austin 7 was licensed internationally (e.g., as the BMW Dixi in Germany, and built under license in the US and Japan), and widely imported into New Zealand, where it was extremely well suited to rural roads and the growing middle class. It remains a favourite among vintage car enthusiasts in NZ.
