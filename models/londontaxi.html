Austin London Taxicab

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin London Taxicab was one of the most iconic models produced by the Austin Motor Company, specifically designed for use as a taxi in London and other cities. It became widely recognized as the quintessential London cab, with its distinctive design and functionality making it the preferred choice for taxi operators.

Introduced in 1929, this taxicab was built to handle the rigorous demands of daily city driving, offering both reliability and comfort for passengers and drivers alike. The Austin London Taxicab quickly became a symbol of urban transport in the UK, particularly in London, where it became a familiar sight on the streets for decades.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 4-cylinder, side-valve
  • Displacement: Approximately 2.2 liters
  • Power Output: Approximately 20 horsepower
  • Transmission: 3-speed manual
  • Chassis: Reinforced frame designed for high-mileage use
  • Weight: Approximately 1,200 kg (2,645 lbs)

The Austin London Taxicab was powered by a reliable 4-cylinder engine that provided enough power for city driving while maintaining fuel efficiency. The chassis was reinforced to withstand the heavy daily use typical for taxis, and its spacious interior was designed to comfortably accommodate both the driver and passengers for long shifts and rides through the busy city streets.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin London Taxicab (1929-1939): The original design, featuring the iconic rear-hinged passenger doors and spacious cabin. This version was widely used in London and other cities during its production run.

The Austin London Taxicab underwent several modifications during its production years to improve comfort, durability, and overall performance, but the core design and functionality remained the same. It became a hallmark of London’s iconic transport system.

The Austin London Taxicab in New Zealand

The Austin London Taxicab, while most famous in the UK, also found its way to New Zealand. It was primarily used in urban areas, particularly in larger cities like Auckland and Wellington, where its spacious design and practicality made it ideal for commercial use. In New Zealand, the Austin London Taxicab was often used by taxi operators and businesses looking for a reliable vehicle to transport groups of passengers or goods.

Although the model never reached the iconic status it achieved in London, it became an important part of New Zealand’s taxi fleet in the early to mid-20th century. Today, it remains a popular model among vintage car collectors and enthusiasts who appreciate its unique history and design.
