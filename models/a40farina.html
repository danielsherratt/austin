Austin A40 Farina (1958–1967)

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin A40 Farina was introduced in 1958 as a significant redesign of the earlier A40 models. The A40 Farina was part of the Farina series of cars designed by the famous Italian coachbuilder Pininfarina. The car offered a more modern and streamlined design, making it one of the first compact cars to blend contemporary styling with good performance and comfort.

The A40 Farina was marketed as a family car with advanced features for its time, combining style with practicality. The car was produced in both saloon and estate variants and became popular in the UK and international markets, including New Zealand, where it was appreciated for its affordability and performance.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 4-cylinder, inline
  • Displacement: 1.2 liters
  • Power Output: 44 horsepower
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Chassis: Unibody design with improved suspension
  • Weight: Approximately 880 kg (1,943 lbs)

The A40 Farina was powered by a 4-cylinder engine, providing a balance of power and fuel efficiency for daily driving. The unibody construction and improved suspension system gave the car a smooth and stable ride, while its stylish design made it stand out on the road. The car’s compact size made it ideal for navigating tight city streets while also offering comfort for longer trips.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin A40 Farina Saloon: The most common variant, offering a spacious interior with modern styling.
  • Austin A40 Farina Estate: A wagon version with increased cargo capacity for those needing more space.

The A40 Farina was offered in both saloon and estate variants. The saloon was the most popular, offering a balanced mix of comfort, style, and practicality. The estate version provided additional cargo space, making it ideal for families or businesses that needed a more versatile vehicle.

The Austin A40 Farina in New Zealand

The A40 Farina was popular in New Zealand due to its blend of style, economy, and practicality. It was used by families, small businesses, and commuters who appreciated its reliability and fuel efficiency. The car’s compact size made it suitable for both urban and rural driving, and its attractive design ensured that it remained a favorite on New Zealand roads for years.

Today, the Austin A40 Farina is considered a classic car in New Zealand, with several well-maintained examples found in vintage car clubs and displayed at car shows.
