Austin 16HP (1939)

Introduction and Historical Significance

The Austin 16HP, introduced in 1939, was a luxury vehicle designed to cater to customers seeking more space, power, and comfort. It was aimed at both private buyers and businesses needing a premium vehicle for personal or commercial use. The Austin 16HP's smooth performance and elegant design made it a popular choice during its time.

During WWII, the 16HP was also adapted for military use, serving as a transport vehicle for various tasks. Its post-war reputation continued as one of the leading British luxury cars of the era.

Technical Specifications

  • Engine: 6-cylinder, side-valve
  • Displacement: Approximately 2.5 liters
  • Power Output: 16 horsepower
  • Transmission: 4-speed manual
  • Chassis: Reinforced frame with better suspension
  • Weight: Approximately 1,250 kg (2,755 lbs)

The Austin 16HP was designed with a more powerful engine compared to earlier models, providing better performance for city and long-distance driving. Its suspension system was also improved to ensure greater comfort for passengers, making it a reliable and refined option for families and business owners alike.

Variant Timeline

  • Austin 16HP Saloon: The main body style, offering luxury and comfort.
  • Austin 16HP Tourer: An open-top variant designed for more leisurely and recreational driving.

The Austin 16HP came in two primary variants, with the saloon being more popular for practical use and the tourer providing a luxury open-top experience.

The Austin 16HP in New Zealand

The Austin 16HP was well-received in New Zealand as a luxury vehicle, mainly used by affluent families and businesses. It became a symbol of status and wealth, and many examples were used for long-distance travel across New Zealand's varied landscape.
